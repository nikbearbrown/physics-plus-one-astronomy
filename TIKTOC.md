## D3 simulation character: Scale and Narrative

Astronomy simulations make incomprehensible scales tangible. The best simulations in this volume will give the student genuine vertigo — the feeling of scale that no prose can fully supply. Every simulation should answer "how big?" or "how far?" or "how long ago?" in a way that lands viscerally.

---

## Curation note

The source book has 30 chapters. This volume curates to ~14. The selection criterion: chapters that (a) have the strongest Attenborough × Feynman cold opens, (b) connect most directly to the physics in the other volumes, and (c) have the most natural D3 simulation. The solar system chapters (ch7–14) are rich but mostly covered in high school; use sparingly.

## Proposed chapter selection (author to confirm)

### Chapter 1 — Science and the Universe (source: ch1)

**D3 simulation:** Scale of the universe — interactive zoom from human scale to observable universe; log-scale navigator
**Core concepts:** Scientific method applied to astronomy, orders of magnitude, light-year, lookback time

### Chapter 2 — Observing the Sky (source: ch2)

**D3 simulation:** Celestial sphere — show altitude/azimuth vs RA/declination; show how sky rotates with latitude slider; show precession
**Core concepts:** Celestial coordinates, seasons, ecliptic, precession, historical astronomy

### Chapter 3 — Radiation and Spectra (source: ch5)

**D3 simulation:** Spectrum analyzer — show blackbody curves at different temperatures; show absorption/emission lines; identify elements from spectral fingerprints
**Core concepts:** Blackbody radiation, Wien's law, Stefan-Boltzmann law, spectral lines, Doppler shift of spectra

### Chapter 4 — The Solar System — Overview (source: ch7 + ch14)

**D3 simulation:** Solar system scale model — show true relative distances (not the textbook diagram which is never to scale); dwarf planet comparator
**Core concepts:** Formation of the solar system, solar system structure, Kepler's laws

### Chapter 5 — The Sun (source: ch15 + ch16)

**D3 simulation:** Solar interior — layers, energy transport, proton-proton chain; solar cycle — sunspot number vs time
**Core concepts:** Solar structure, nuclear fusion, solar activity, solar wind, space weather

### Chapter 6 — Analyzing Starlight (source: ch17 + ch18)

**D3 simulation:** H-R diagram builder — plot stars by luminosity and temperature; show main sequence, giants, white dwarfs; click star to show properties
**Core concepts:** Stellar classification, H-R diagram, luminosity classes, color-temperature relation

### Chapter 7 — Stellar Distances and the Cosmic Distance Ladder (source: ch19)

**D3 simulation:** Distance ladder — show each rung (parallax → Cepheid → Type Ia → Hubble); show uncertainty growing at each step; connect to H₀ tension
**Core concepts:** Parallax, Cepheid variables, standard candles, distance modulus, cosmic distance ladder

### Chapter 8 — The Birth of Stars (source: ch21)

**D3 simulation:** Star-forming region — show molecular cloud, Jeans instability, protostar formation, T Tauri phase; show exoplanet detection methods (transit, radial velocity)
**Core concepts:** Star formation, protostellar evolution, exoplanet detection, habitable zone

### Chapter 9 — Stars from Adolescence to Old Age (source: ch22)

**D3 simulation:** Stellar evolution track on H-R diagram — animate main sequence lifetime, then red giant branch, then endpoint; vary initial mass to show different tracks
**Core concepts:** Main sequence lifetime, red giant phase, mass-luminosity relation, stellar nucleosynthesis

### Chapter 10 — The Death of Stars (source: ch23)

**D3 simulation:** Stellar death router — input mass, watch endpoint: planetary nebula + white dwarf, or core collapse supernova + neutron star/black hole; show Chandrasekhar limit
**Core concepts:** White dwarfs, planetary nebulae, supernovae, neutron stars, black hole formation

### Chapter 11 — Black Holes and Curved Spacetime (source: ch24)

**D3 simulation:** Spacetime curvature visualization — rubber sheet analogy (noting its limits); geodesic paths; gravitational redshift; event horizon properties
**Core concepts:** General relativity (qualitative), Schwarzschild radius, gravitational waves, first black hole image

### Chapter 12 — The Milky Way and Galaxies (source: ch25 + ch26)

**D3 simulation:** Galaxy morphology classifier — show elliptical, spiral, irregular; Milky Way structure from above; rotation curve — show dark matter evidence
**Core concepts:** Milky Way structure, galaxy types, dark matter evidence from rotation curves, galaxy clusters

### Chapter 13 — The Big Bang (source: ch29)

**D3 simulation:** Hubble diagram with real data — plot galaxies, fit recession velocity vs distance; CMB blackbody spectrum at 2.7K; universe timeline from Big Bang to now
**Core concepts:** Hubble's law, cosmic expansion, Big Bang evidence (CMB, nucleosynthesis, expansion), dark energy

### Chapter 14 — Life in the Universe (source: ch30)

**D3 simulation:** Drake equation interactive — vary all parameters, watch N over enormous range; habitable zone calculator — vary stellar type and planet distance; SETI signal search visualizer
**Core concepts:** Conditions for life, Drake equation, exoplanet habitability, SETI, Fermi paradox
