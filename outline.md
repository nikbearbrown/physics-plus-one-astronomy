## Introduction

The universe at every scale. Astronomy chapter selection prioritizes (a) the strongest Attenborough × Feynman cold opens, (b) the chapters that connect to physics in the other volumes, (c) the most natural D3 simulations. Solar-system chapters are used sparingly; the high-leverage chapters are about stars, galaxies, and cosmology.

## Chapters (proposed — author to confirm curation)

1. **Science and the Universe** — D3 scale-of-the-universe zoom; lookback time, light-year, orders of magnitude.
2. **Observing the Sky** — D3 celestial sphere; coordinates, seasons, precession, historical astronomy.
3. **Radiation and Spectra** — D3 spectrum analyzer; blackbody, Wien, Stefan-Boltzmann, Doppler shift.
4. **The Solar System — Overview** — D3 true-scale solar system; formation, Kepler's laws.
5. **The Sun** — D3 solar interior + cycle; nuclear fusion, solar wind, space weather.
6. **Analyzing Starlight** — D3 H-R diagram builder; stellar classification, luminosity classes.
7. **Stellar Distances and the Cosmic Distance Ladder** — D3 distance ladder with uncertainty; parallax through Hubble, H₀ tension.
8. **The Birth of Stars** — D3 star-forming region + exoplanet detection methods.
9. **Stars from Adolescence to Old Age** — D3 stellar evolution tracks on H-R diagram.
10. **The Death of Stars** — D3 stellar death router; white dwarfs, neutron stars, black holes.
11. **Black Holes and Curved Spacetime** — D3 spacetime curvature; event horizon, gravitational waves, M87*.
12. **The Milky Way and Galaxies** — D3 galaxy morphology + rotation curves (dark matter evidence).
13. **The Big Bang** — D3 Hubble diagram + CMB spectrum; expanding universe, dark energy.
14. **Life in the Universe** — D3 Drake equation interactive; habitable zone, SETI, Fermi paradox.
